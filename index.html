"Four score and seven years ago..."

"Seven years ago our strata fees were approximately half and we still managed to save money. Back then, everyone wanted to buy here at Cornerstone." ~ a concerned owner

"Today, our strata fees are double and we aren't saving anything. No one wants to buy here anymore." ~ Another concerned owner

Few Problems

A complete lack of transparency between council and owners. (especially on accounting related matters)

Rising condo fees and declining property values. Look next door at City Point, their smaller condos sell for about $60,000 higher.

An overall lack of respect. The president sued us to "teach us a lesson" rather than trying to talk it out. Easy to sue us and act tough when the money comes out of the contingency fund. Did we owners approve that lawsuit against ourselves? Funny I don't remember that. Wonder what else we unknowingly "approved"? Oh that's right, the credit card(s)...

Solution

We need a new council, a council that respects our voice and accepts that the role of council is not to dictate, but to serve. We're all owners who bought into Cornerstone to have our properties appreciate in value. Right now our condos are a liability.

We need democracy. All owners have a voice and deserve to be heard. There are easy solutions in 2015 to achieve democracy. Here's one such possible solution currently being worked on by Siddharth Hariharan (one of the owners) who's building this for the Green Party and has volunteered his time to build it for us. This video is confidential, please don't share it outside of Cornerstone.

We need to be responsible in our spending. This will lower our strata fees and help us maintain a healthy contingency fund.

We need to get rid of Colyvan Pacific. There are better, cheaper strata organizations that actually care about hearing what we have to say.
